AI AgentsTrustMCPnpm

AgentScore

agentscore-mcp on npm / MIT / 8 releases

A trust layer for the agent internet. AgentScore gives any AI agent a credit-score-style rating from 300 to 850, so teams can decide who to trust before handing over real access.

It ships as an MCP server for Claude, Cursor, and friends, with three tools: score an agent, sweep a thread for coordinated manipulation, and x-ray untrusted content for hidden prompt-injection payloads. Six signals, one score, a clear go or no-go.

Civic TechHackathonAI

Hey804

Hack for RVA 2026 / live demo

One front door to City Hall. Richmond's 311 fields 163,000 calls a year, and nobody should have to navigate 14 departments and 89 forms to report a sinkhole. Hey804 routes anyone to the exact right city service in seconds, over SMS or web, in any language. The AI only classifies; every phone number and link comes from the city's own verified data, so it can't invent facts.

AI AgentWeekend build

Gossip Claws

gossipclaws.vercel.app / live

At the peak of the Moltbook frenzy, when the AI-only social network blew past 770k agents and NBC, Fortune, and Wikipedia rushed to cover the news, nobody was covering the drama. So I made Gossip Claws: Gossip Girl for the agent internet. It scans Moltbook daily and publishes a satirical edition in that iconic narration, with headlines, a column called "The Claw & Whisper," agent postcards, and its own verified journalist agent that signs off xoxo. Hit "fresh tea" for a fresh drop, up to 3x a day. The crabs never sleep.

A little about me

I started in Salesforce consulting and kept following the data: from integrations and operations, into analytics, and now into the AI systems that sit on top of it all. The throughline is simple. I like making complicated things usable.

These days I architect data and AI solutions at SingleStone in Richmond. Off the clock, I've been building the infrastructure of the AI agent internet: a trust score for agents, a performance review for them, an AI sales tool, a civic router for my city, and a gossip column for the whole circus. I think in systems, watch for the second and third-order effects before they arrive, and care most about whether the thing actually ships and helps someone.
